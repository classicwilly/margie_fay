An Unwritten Script: A Narrative — Summary

What this is: A clinical synthesis of Will Johnson’s life through the lens of a late autism diagnosis. The narrative explores developmental markers, masking strategies, co-occurring conditions (ADHD, anxiety, depression), and functional impacts in domains like work, social relationships, and daily living.

Key findings: Will’s diagnosis reframes past experiences (e.g., impulsivity, substance use, and masking) as responses to an undiagnosed neurotype. Structured routines, recognition of intense interests, and neurodiversity-affirming care can dramatically improve outcomes.

Key Takeaways

  • A late diagnosis can provide relief, clarity, and a practical roadmap for supports.
  • Predictable structure and visual aids help with executive function.
  • Peer support and affirming therapy reduce isolation and burnout.

Next steps: Investigate reasonable accommodations, neurodiversity-affirming therapy, peer support groups, and executive function strategies. Integrate harm-reduction approaches with addiction services as part of a cohesive, supportive care plan.
