## Student Powers by Configuration

### Neurotypical Students
**You are in control.**

**Powers:**
- ✅ **Control all data sharing** - Choose who sees what (grades, comprehension, emotional data)
- ✅ **Choose study buddy** - Pick peer you trust, not assigned by teacher
- ✅ **Lead conferences** - Present your progress, propose goals, adults listen
- ✅ **Set privacy rules** - Decide what parents see, what stays private
- ✅ **Request extensions** - System tracks workload, you can ask for more time
- ✅ **Veto interventions** - "I don't need help with this" = respected

**Example:**
You're doing well in AP Calc but struggling with essays. You share calc data with study buddy (shows mastery), share writing data with tutor (shows struggle), hide emotional stress from parents (not ready to talk about it). **You control the flow.**

---

### Neurodivergent Students (ADHD/Autism/Dyslexia)
**Your IEP, your rules.**

**Powers:**
- ✅ **Lead IEP meetings** - You present: "Extended time helps. Separate room makes me feel singled out. I want front row seat instead."
- ✅ **Veto accommodations** - "I tried the graphic organizer. It doesn't work for my brain. Can we try recording instead?"
- ✅ **Control disclosure** - Decide which teachers know diagnosis, which peers, what details shared
- ✅ **Choose specialist** - Find therapist/tutor you trust, not assigned
- ✅ **Decide on strategies** - Teacher proposes executive function tools, you test and approve
- ✅ **Manage medication privacy** - Parents/doctor know, but you decide if teachers know

**Example:**
You have ADHD. IEP meeting: You show data proving timers stress you out, but breaking tasks into 3 steps works. Teacher agrees to drop timers, implement chunking. You tell math teacher about ADHD (she's supportive), don't tell gym teacher (he doesn't get it). **Your brain, your strategy, your choice.**

---

### Struggling Students
**You are NOT broken. You are learning differently.**

**Powers:**
- ✅ **Track own progress** - You see words-per-minute increasing (baseline 45 → current 78), **you celebrate wins**
- ✅ **Set own goals** - "I want 75 words/min by my birthday" (not teacher's generic goal)
- ✅ **Veto ineffective methods** - "Flashcards don't work for me. Can we try songs?"
- ✅ **Choose tutoring focus** - "I want to work on comprehension, not fluency anymore"
- ✅ **Decide who knows** - "Don't tell other kids I have a tutor" = respected
- ✅ **Co-design lessons** - Tutor asks: "What should we work on today?" You answer.

**Example:**
You're 2 years behind in reading. Tutor shows you progress chart (you've gained 33 words/min in 3 months). You: "I can read fast now but don't remember what I read." Tutor: "Let's focus on comprehension strategies." You co-design next 4 weeks. **You own your learning journey.**

---

### Gifted Students
**Boredom is not a character flaw. It's underchallenge.**

**Powers:**
- ✅ **Design own curriculum** - "I've finished algebra 2. Can I do calculus?" Teacher: "Show me your plan." You design it.
- ✅ **Veto busywork** - "I understand multiplication. I don't need 50 practice problems." Teacher: "Do 5 to show mastery, then move on."
- ✅ **Teach peers** - Give mini-lessons on topics you've mastered (builds leadership, deepens understanding)
- ✅ **Propose projects** - "Can I build a black hole simulation instead of the solar system poster?"
- ✅ **Find own mentor** - You research local college profs, reach out, arrange monthly meetings
- ✅ **Choose challenge level** - You tell teacher: "Too easy" or "Perfect stretch" or "Over my head"

**Example:**
You're obsessed with astrophysics. You propose: "Can I skip algebra 2 and do Khan Academy calculus?" Teacher: "Yes, but you need 80%+ on all quizzes." You design study plan, meet with college physics prof monthly (you found them), teach classmates about relativity (builds your teaching skills). **You own your education.**

## Privacy Controls (All Students)

### What Students Control:

**Grades:**
- ❌ Default: Private
- ✅ Student chooses: Share with parents? (Yes/No)
- ✅ Student chooses: Share with study buddy? (Yes/No/Specific subjects only)
- ✅ Student chooses: Share with teacher? (Always visible to teacher for grading, but student can hide from others)

**Comprehension Scores (Daily Check-ins):**
- ❌ Default: Private
- ✅ Student chooses: Share with teacher? (Recommended yes for help, but can opt out)
- ✅ Student chooses: Share with parents? (Student decides)
- ✅ Student chooses: Anonymous in class heatmap? (Yes by default, opt-in to reveal name)

**Emotional Check-ins (Mood, anxiety, stress):**
- ❌ Default: PRIVATE (student only)
- ✅ Student chooses: Share with parents? (Yes/No)
- ✅ Student chooses: Share with counselor? (Yes/No)
- ⚠️ Guardian Node AI sees patterns (detects crisis) but doesn't share details without student permission
- ⚠️ Exception: If Guardian Node detects self-harm risk, alerts counselor (safety override)

**Study Session Data:**
- ❌ Default: Private
- ✅ Student chooses: Share with tutor? (Helps them see what strategies work)
- ✅ Student chooses: Share with parents? (Student decides)

**IEP/504 Diagnosis:**
- ❌ Default: PRIVATE
- ✅ Student chooses: Which teachers know?
- ✅ Student chooses: Which peers know?
- ✅ Student chooses: What details shared? ("I have ADHD" vs "I take medication" vs full diagnosis)

## Student Veto Scenarios

### Veto 1: Accommodations
**Scenario:** Teacher implements IEP accommodation: separate room for tests.

**Student:** "Extended time helps, but separate room makes me feel singled out. I want to stay in classroom, just sit in back corner with noise-canceling headphones."

**Result:** Teacher updates accommodation plan. **Student's lived experience > adult assumptions.**

---

### Veto 2: Tutoring Approach
**Scenario:** Tutor uses flashcards for vocabulary.

**Student:** "Flashcards don't work for me. I forget the words 5 minutes later. Can we try making silly songs?"

**Result:** Tutor switches to music-based learning. Student's memory improves. **Student knows their brain better than tutor does.**

---

### Veto 3: Parent Over-involvement
**Scenario:** Parent wants daily progress reports (checking every assignment grade).

**Student:** "Mom, this stresses me out. I feel like I'm being watched all the time. Can we do weekly check-ins instead?"

**System:** Student adjusts parent's access level to weekly summaries only.

**Result:** Student feels trusted, still gets parent support. **Student controls boundaries.**

---

### Veto 4: Peer Tutoring
**Scenario:** Teacher assigns peer tutor (student performing well in class).

**Student:** "I don't want peer tutoring right now. I need to figure this out on my own first."

**Result:** Teacher respects decision, checks in 2 weeks later. **Student's autonomy > teacher's plan.**

---

### Veto 5: Busywork
**Scenario:** Gifted student assigned 50 multiplication problems (already mastered).

**Student:** "I understand multiplication. Can I do 5 problems to prove mastery, then move to division?"

**Teacher:** "Show me 5 problems. 100% correct? You're done."

**Result:** Student spends time on actual learning, not compliance theater. **Student's time is respected.**

## Student-Led Conferences

### Traditional Parent-Teacher Conference:
- Student absent (or sitting silently)
- Teacher talks to parents: "Johnny is struggling with..."
- Parents: "We'll talk to him."
- **Student is OBJECT of discussion, not participant.**

### Student-Led Conference in G.O.D. Mesh:

**Student presents:**
1. **Progress data:** "Here's my math grade over time (graph). I was at 65% in September, now I'm at 82%."
2. **What's working:** "Breaking homework into 3 chunks with 10-min breaks helps me focus."
3. **What's not working:** "The reading log feels like busywork. Can I write summaries instead?"
4. **Goals:** "I want to get to 90% by end of semester. I think I need help with word problems."
5. **Request for support:** "Mom, can you quiz me on vocab 3x/week? Teacher, can I come to office hours Tuesdays?"

**Adults:**
- Teacher: "I agree. Let's add office hours. Here's what I see in class..."
- Parent: "Yes, I can do vocab quizzes. How can I help with word problems?"

**Result:** Student owns narrative. Adults support student's plan, don't impose their plan on student.

## Implementation: Start Small

### Level 1: Privacy Controls
- Let students choose who sees grades (start with gradebook visibility)
- Let students opt-in/opt-out of class heatmaps (start with anonymous by default)

### Level 2: Veto Power (Low Stakes)
- Let students veto seating arrangements ("I focus better away from the window")
- Let students choose homework format (worksheet vs flashcards vs video summary)

### Level 3: Student-Led Check-ins
- Weekly 5-min meeting: "What's working? What's not? What do you need?"
- Student talks, adults listen, student decides next steps

### Level 4: Student-Led Conferences
- Student prepares presentation (5 slides: progress, strategies, goals, requests)
- Student presents to parents + teacher
- Adults ask clarifying questions, offer support

### Level 5: Student-Designed Curriculum
- Gifted students: Design independent study plan, find mentor, teach peers
- Struggling students: Choose tutoring focus, co-design intervention
- Neurodivergent students: Lead IEP, choose accommodations
